Lesson 1 1

What Does Sound Look Like?

Computing · Amazing Africa

You say

Welcome — today we find out how a computer can see sound. Don't explain yet. Scroll to the next slide when ready.

Hook · 5 mins 2 visibility

Watch the screen.

Say nothing — play your sound clip with the wave visible.

Do now

Play the audio clip (quiet → loud). Let pupils watch the wave grow. Do not explain. When it finishes, go to the next slide and ask the question.

Hook 3

What did you notice about the picture when the sound got louder?

Look for

Pupils describe the wave getting bigger / spikier. Take 2–3 answers. Don't correct yet — just listen. Follow-up slide if needed.

Hook 4

What do you think that picture is showing us?

You say

Take a few ideas. Still don't give the full answer — you're building curiosity. Then: "Let's find out."

Explain · 8 mins 5

Can a computer see sound?

You say

Hands up — yes, no, not sure? Open SoundWave on the board (link top-right). You're going to show them the answer live.

Explain · Demo 6 hearing

Watch the wave.

Tap the blue button · don't speak yet · then whisper · then talk · then stay quiet.

Do now

Demo on SoundWave — blue button only (Listen). Point when flat. Whisper, normal voice, silence. Ask after each: "What changed?" Nothing is saved.

Explain · Vocabulary 7

microphone

It listens — and turns sound into data the computer can keep.

You say

Point at the mic / iPad. "The microphone hears you and the computer stores it as data — not on paper, inside the device."

Explain · Vocabulary 8

sound wave

The line on the screen that shows us the sound.

You say

Point at the orange line. "This shape is a sound wave — it's the computer drawing the data so we can see it."

Explain · Vocabulary 9

digital

Saved on a device — not on paper, not in the air.

You say

"Digital means the sound is stored inside the computer or iPad as information." Check: wave when you stop talking?

Explain · Check 10

What happens to the wave when I stop talking?

Look for

It goes flat / still. Then ask: "What would a really LOUD sound look like?" (bigger peaks). Optional: has anyone seen this on a phone?

Reflect · 8 mins 22

devices Devices down

Do now

Headphones off. Ask 2–3 pairs to share on the board — play their clip, point at the wave together.

Reflect 23

Can a computer see sound?

Establish

Yes — it turns sound into data and draws a wave. The wave tells us about the sound before we press play. Point at features: where they started, quiet gaps.

Reflect · Exit 24

Loud clap or quiet whisper — which wave has bigger peaks?

Show of hands

Loud = bigger peaks. Then next slide for digital.

Reflect · Exit 25

What does digital mean?
